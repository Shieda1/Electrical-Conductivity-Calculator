What is the electrical conductivity?
The electrical conductivity is a fundimental property of a material that measures how strongly it resists electric current. It is represented by the greek letter ρ.
How to calculate electrical conductivity?
To calculate electrical conductivity you should use the following formula:
R = (l / A) * ρ
where:
R = conductivity
l = distance
A = area
ρ = specific resistance
Why use electrical conductivity?
The electrical conductivity is used in order to measure when undertaking environmental or process monitoring.
